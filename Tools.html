US Visas

The United States offers various types of visas for different purposes, including study, work, business, and tourism. Each visa type has specific requirements, validity periods, and regulations.

An F1 visa is a nonimmigrant visa for those wishing to study in the U.S. It is intended for full-time students and is not designed as a work visa.
F2 visa is the dependent visa of the F1 visa, which allows spouses and children (unmarried under 21 years old) to enter the USA along with the F1 visa holder.
Curricular Practical Training (CPT) is a program that temporarily allows international students with an F-1 visa to gain practical experience directly related to their major through employment.
Optional Practical Training (OPT) is a period during which undergraduate and graduate students with F-1 status can work towards getting practical training to complement their field of studies.
H1B visa is a work visa in the USA for temporary employment. It is generally approved for 3 years and can be extended for another 3 years, making its validity 6 years in total.
H4 visa is the dependent visa of the H1B visa. Its validity can be extended depending on the spouse's visa status. H4 visa holders can also work in the USA after receiving an Employment Authorization Document (EAD).
E3 visa is provided to Australian citizens. It is similar to the H1B visa and is valid for 2 years, but one can apply for an extension.
E3-D visa is the dependent visa of the E3 visa, similar to the dependent visa of the H1B visa (H4). E3-D visa holders can also start working as soon as they receive their Employment Authorization Document (EAD).
TN visa is a work visa in the USA, but it is only for Canadian and Mexican citizens. It is valid for 3 years.
TN TD visa is the dependent visa of the TN visa, similar to the dependent visa of the H1B visa (H4) and E3 visa (E3-D).
L1 Visa is an inter-company transfer visa, initially made for large multinational companies to transfer their employees to the USA for temporary purposes.
L2 visa holders are dependents of L1 visa holders. They can start working after receiving their Employment Authorization Document (EAD).
Having a Green Card or becoming a Green Card holder means being officially known as a permanent resident, which allows one to live and work permanently in the USA.
B1 visa is a business visa that allows foreigners to enter the USA for business purposes.
B2 visa is a visiting visa for tourism, which allows foreigners to visit the USA for non-business purposes.
US citizens are permanent residents of the USA and are authorized to work in the country with any employer.

Understanding Tax Terms in US Staffing

When it comes to US staffing, understanding tax terms is crucial for both employers and employees. Here are some common tax terms:

  • W-2: This tax term involves an agreement between an individual and a corporation. Only Green card holders, US citizens, EAD holders, or TN Visa holders can work on W-2 tax terms. W-2 candidates are entitled to basic benefits like medical insurance, PTO, and 401K.
  • C2C: C2C is an agreement between two corporations, not individuals. Tax is paid by the corporation on behalf of their consultants. H1B visa holders often work on C2C terms, where the staffing firm markets their resume.
  • 1099: This tax term is rarely used in the US job market. Individuals paid on 1099 terms are responsible for paying their own taxes as all earnings are paid untaxed.

Common Tax Terms in US Staffing

In addition to the above, here are some other tax terms commonly used in US staffing:

  • W-2 Employee: An individual considered a common law employee with taxes withheld by the employer.
  • 1099 Independent Contractor: An individual considered self-employed responsible for their own taxes.
  • Corp to Corp: Used when a candidate works on behalf of an employer for another client, with the employer responsible for taxes and wages.
  • Payroll Taxes: Taxes withheld from an employees pay and remitted to the government by the employer.
  • Unemployment Taxes: Taxes paid by employers to fund unemployment insurance programs.
  • Federal Income Tax: Taxes paid by individuals on their earned income to the federal government.
  • State Income Tax: Taxes paid by individuals on their earned income to the state government.
  • FICA: Federal Insurance Contributions Act, funding Social Security and Medicare programs.
  • Withholding: Deducting money from an employees pay for taxes.
